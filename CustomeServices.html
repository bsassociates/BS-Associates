What is Customs Duty?
Definition: Customs duty is an indirect tax levied on all goods and commodities imported to India and certain goods exported from India.
Import and Export: It applies to both imports and exports, ensuring fair competition and protecting domestic producers.
Types of Customs Duty:
  • Basic Customs Duty (BCD): Levied as a percentage of the assessable value of imported goods.
  • Additional Customs Duty (ACD): Equivalent to the excise duty on similar locally produced goods.
  • Education Cess and Secondary and Higher Education Cess: Additional charges for education development.
  • Anti-Dumping Duty: Imposed on goods priced below market value to protect domestic producers1.
Customs Duty Refund and Drawback:

Refund: Importers can receive refunds for overpaid customs duties, taxes, and fees.
Drawback: Refund of duties paid on exported goods or inputs.
Transfer of Residency: Returning residents can benefit from relaxed customs rules for personal belongings. Remember, understanding customs duty is crucial for international trade and compliance. If you have more questions, feel free to ask!
