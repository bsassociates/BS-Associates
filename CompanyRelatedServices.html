Registered Companies:
Definition: Registered companies come into existence only after receiving a Certificate of Incorporation from the Registrar of Companies (RoC) under the Companies Act, 2013.
Features:
  • Perpetual Succession: A registered company remains unaffected by death, insolvency, or losses. It is a separate legal entity.
  • Limited Liability: Members’ liability is limited to the extent of their shareholding.
  • Easy Share Transfers: Shares can be transferred freely.
  • Professional Management: The board of directors manages the company.
Statutory Companies:
Definition: Statutory companies are formed by special acts passed by the Parliament or central/state legislation. They exercise control over specific areas or commercial activities.
Features:
  • Owned by the State: These companies are state-owned and can enter contracts or start businesses in their name.
  • Separate Staffing System: Employees are not government servants and follow specific rules.
  • Financial Independence: They can borrow money within and outside the country with government permission.
