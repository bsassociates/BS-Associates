Certainly! Let’s dive into the world of Goods and Services Tax (GST) and understand how GST returns work:
What is a GST Return? A GST return is a document that contains details of a taxpayer’s sales, purchases, and tax liability under the GST regime. It serves as evidence of transactions entered into by a person registered under GST.
Types of GST Returns: There are several types of GST returns, each serving a specific purpose.
Here are some key ones:
  • GSTR-1:Details of outward supplies (sales) made by the taxpayer.
  • GSTR-3B:Summary of monthly tax liability and input tax credit (ITC) claimed.
  • GSTR-4:For composition scheme taxpayers.
  • GSTR-5:For non-resident foreign taxpayers.
  • GSTR-6:Input Service Distributor’s return.
  • GSTR-7:Return for tax deducted at source (TDS).
  • GSTR-8:E-commerce operators’ return.
  • GSTR-9:Annual return.
  • GSTR-10:Final return for canceled registrations.
  • GSTR-11:Details of inward supplies received by UIN holders.
  • CMP-08:For composition dealers.
  • ITC-04:Details of goods sent to job workers.
The due dates for filing these returns vary based on the type of taxpayer and the frequency of filing.
Who Should File GST Returns? Regular businesses with an annual aggregate turnover exceeding ₹5 crore must file two monthly returns and one annual return. Taxpayers with a turnover of up to ₹5 crore can opt for the Quarterly Return Monthly Payment (QRMP) scheme, filing 9 returns annually. Special cases (e.g., composition dealers) have separate return requirements1. Remember, accurate and timely GST return filing ensures compliance and helps you manage your tax obligations effectively.
